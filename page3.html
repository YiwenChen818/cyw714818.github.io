In The Witch's House, the grandmother prepares a special dish for her granddaughter: Herring and Pumpkin Pot Pie. Add to Meal Planner Ingredients: Pumpkin about 300g Garlic 2 cloves Flour 2 tablespoons Egg 1 Onion 1 Potatoes 2 Milk or cream 1 cup Salt and pepper moderate
a. Processing the pumpkin and potatoes: Boil the pumpkin cubes and potato slices in salted water until slightly softened, drain and set aside.
b. Sauté the filling: Heat the butter in a pan, add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until the onion is soft. Add the pumpkin and potatoes and continue to sauté for 2-3 minutes until the ingredients are evenly heated. Add the cubed herring to the pan and sauté gently for a few minutes until it has browned. Season with salt, pepper, and thyme.
c. Make the sauce: Melt the butter in a separate pan, add the flour and stir until well mixed. Slowly add the milk or cream, whisking constantly, until the sauce has thickened. Pour the sauce over the sautéed filling and stir well to coat all the ingredients.
d. Assemble the pie: Lay a pie crust in the bottom of a pie dish and pour in the prepared filling. If you have a leftover pie crust, cut it into strips and weave them into a lattice to cover the filling, or just cover the entire pie crust and cut a few small slits in the top to allow for venting. Brush the pie crust with beaten egg wash to give it a golden colour when baking.
e. Bake: Place the pie in a preheated 350°F (180°C) oven and bake for 30-40 minutes until the crust is golden and crisp.
f. Enjoy: Remove from the oven and allow to cool slightly before slicing and serving. The combination of herring and pumpkin is richly flavoured with a tangy sauce and crispy pie crust. Descriptions

a. Sauté vegetables: Heat olive oil or butter in a large pan. Add the chopped onion and garlic, and sauté until the onion is soft and transparent. The delightful aroma will soon fill your kitchen, adding to the excitement of cooking. Add the chopped carrot and squash pieces and continue to sauté for about 5 minutes to soften the vegetables slightly.
b. Cook the soup: Pour the chicken or vegetable stock, ensuring the vegetables are completely covered. If you like, add sliced ginger and thyme or rosemary to add flavour to the soup. Let the soup boil, turn down the heat, cover and simmer for about 20-30 minutes until the squash and carrots are completely tender.
c. Puree: Using a handheld blender or pouring the soup into a blender, puree the cooked soup into a smooth paste. If you prefer a thicker texture, you can reduce the blending time, leaving some chunks of vegetables behind.
d. Adjust the consistency: To thicken the soup, you can add cream and continue to blend until well combined. Add water or stock in moderation to adjust to your preferred consistency.
e. Season to taste: Season with salt and pepper to taste.
f. Enjoy: Serve the pumpkin soup in bowls and enjoy it with some fresh bread. Descriptions
